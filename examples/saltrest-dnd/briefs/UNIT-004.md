# UNIT-004 — Resolution paths, rewards & consequences

## Objective
Write the endgame: the three distinct resolution paths, what each requires, and the
rewards/consequences of each — tying together the hook, the NPC motives, and the clock.

## Inputs / context
- CONTRACT.md (the three pinned paths and the clock).
- UNIT-001 output `briefs/out/hook.md` (the call to action).
- UNIT-002 output `briefs/out/npcs.md` (NPC motives — esp. Coria's mercy framing).
- UNIT-003 output `briefs/out/dungeon.md` (the clock's exact round numbers and DCs).

## Approach
- Write a `## Resolution` section with one subsection per path (`### Path 1 — …`):
  1. **Combat** — destroy the Choir; what victory and failure look like.
  2. **Town defense** — hold Saltrest's breakwater when the drowned come ashore.
  3. **Mercy / read the ledger** — recover and read the Seraphine ledger aloud
     before it dissolves; reference the clock's real numbers from UNIT-003.
- For each path: the trigger/requirement, the likely outcome, and a concrete reward
  or consequence (item, NPC fate, lasting change to Saltrest). Make the paths lead
  to genuinely different end states (not the same ending reskinned).

## Constraints
- GM-facing, second person, present tense. Honor canon, the NPC motives, and the
  exact clock numbers from UNIT-003 (do not invent different round counts).
- Write only to `briefs/out/resolution.md`.

## Acceptance criteria
- [ ] (assertional) `briefs/out/resolution.md` has `## Resolution` and three path subsections matching the contract's three paths
- [ ] (assertional, player_agency) the three paths lead to genuinely different end states, each with its own requirement/trigger
- [ ] (assertional, specificity) each path names a concrete reward or consequence (an item, an NPC fate, or a lasting change to Saltrest)
- [ ] (assertional, narrative_tension) the mercy path explicitly references the clock and the risk of the ledger dissolving (using UNIT-003's actual numbers)
- [ ] (assertional) consistent with hook.md, npcs.md (Coria's mercy motive), and dungeon.md (clock numbers); no contradictions

## Dependencies
UNIT-001, UNIT-002, UNIT-003
