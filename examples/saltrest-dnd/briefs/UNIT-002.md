# UNIT-002 — NPCs & Factions

## Objective
Write the cast: the named NPCs and the antagonist faction, each with motivation and
an emotional beat a GM can play at the table.

## Inputs / context
- CONTRACT.md (canonical entities and their pinned motives). Use them exactly.

## Approach
- Write a `## Cast & Factions` section. For each, give a short stat-light entry
  (role, what they want, one vivid emotional/physical detail, what they know):
  - **Mayor Halrik** (missing — what actually happened to him).
  - **Mother Vell** (the cleric who refuses the water — why; what she fears).
  - **Coria Sleet** (the necromancer — her mercy-not-malice motive: reading the
    Seraphine ledger to "carry the souls home").
  - **the Drowned Choir** (the raised sailors — what they are, what they want).
- Make at least one NPC a potential ally and at least one a potential antagonist,
  so social approaches are live (supports the negotiation path).

## Constraints
- GM-facing, second person, present tense. Grim nautical folk-horror tone.
- Honor pinned motives — especially Coria's "mercy" framing (she is not a cackling villain).
- Do not write room layouts (UNIT-003) or final rewards (UNIT-004).
- Write only to `briefs/out/npcs.md`.

## Acceptance criteria
- [ ] (assertional) `briefs/out/npcs.md` has a `## Cast & Factions` heading and covers all four: Mayor Halrik, Mother Vell, Coria Sleet, the Drowned Choir
- [ ] (assertional, specificity) each entry has a motivation AND one concrete sensory/emotional detail (a named object, gesture, or line), not generic description
- [ ] (assertional, player_agency) at least one NPC is playable as an ally and one as an antagonist, so social/negotiation approaches are viable
- [ ] (assertional) Coria Sleet's motive matches the contract's "mercy, not malice" framing; no canon contradictions

## Dependencies
none
