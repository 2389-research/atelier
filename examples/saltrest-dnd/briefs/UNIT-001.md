# UNIT-001 — The Hook & Saltrest

## Objective
Write the opening of the adventure: the situation, the village of Saltrest, the
opening scene that lands the party in it, and the clear call to action.

## Inputs / context
- CONTRACT.md (seed premise, canonical entities, tone). Use the exact names.

## Approach
- Write a `## The Hook` section (~200–300 words):
  - Open with a concrete sensory scene in Saltrest (the empty nets, the bone-catch,
    the mood at the Salted Anchor).
  - State plainly what is wrong and what the party is asked to do (find Mayor Halrik
    / stop whatever is in the water).
  - Seed 2–3 distinct investigative threads the party could pull (the necromancer
    sighting, Mother Vell's refusal, the north-shore caves) — do not resolve them.
- Reference only canon entities; do not invent new named NPCs (that's UNIT-002).

## Constraints
- GM-facing, second person, present tense. Grim nautical folk-horror tone.
- Do not describe the dungeon interior (UNIT-003) or final outcomes (UNIT-004).
- Write only to `briefs/out/hook.md`.

## Acceptance criteria
- [ ] (assertional) `briefs/out/hook.md` has a `## The Hook` heading; ~150–320 words
- [ ] (assertional, specificity) uses the canon names Saltrest, Mayor Halrik, and at least one of {Salted Anchor, breakwater, Mother Vell, Coria Sleet}; includes concrete sensory detail (not "a town, a problem")
- [ ] (assertional, hook_clarity) a GM reading it once knows what is wrong and what the party is asked to do
- [ ] (assertional, player_agency) seeds ≥2 distinct investigative threads, not a single rail
- [ ] (assertional) no contradictions with the seed premise; no new named NPCs invented

## Dependencies
none
