## The Hook

You arrive at Saltrest on a gray morning when the fishing boats are being hosed down with seawater and salt. Something is wrong here.

The nets that went out three weeks ago have come up empty or lined with something worse—human bones, small and bleached, tangled in the cordage like a macabre catch. The fishers won't talk about it much beyond short, angry words. They just want the nets burned. At the Salted Anchor, the tavern's common room is half-full of people nursing drinks in daylight, and the mood is one of coiled dread. Someone is missing: **Mayor Halrik**, the village leader, rowed out alone last Thursday toward the **sea caves on the north shore**—the ones the locals call **Kelpwater Cove**—and never returned. His widow waits at his house. The village's guards have not gone looking.

**Mother Vell**, the priest who tends to the shrine of the drowned saint, has been seen at her temple since it happened, and when asked if she will help, she refuses flatly: no prayer will take her near the water. Her hands shake when she speaks of it.

The day before the mayor vanished, a **necromancer named Coria Sleet** was spotted at the market buying rope, lanterns, and wax—supplies for something underground and lengthy. The merchant remembers her because she was kind, almost gentle, asking after the caves as if planning a scholarly expedition. She left town after that. No one has seen her since.

The party is asked directly: find Mayor Halrik. Stop whatever is killing the fishery. The pay is modest—what the village can afford—but the stakes are clear. If the bones keep coming and the water stays dead, Saltrest will starve by winter.

Three threads pull the party in different directions: the caves themselves, waiting in the cliffs; the mystery of the necromancer's purpose and present whereabouts; and Mother Vell's terror, which hints at something unholy already known.
