## Resolution

The adventure's three distinct paths emerge from the Vault's ticking clock and the Drowned Choir's singular purpose. Each leads to a fundamentally different end state for Saltrest, its survivors, and the drowned souls seeking peace.

### Path 1 — Combat / Descend and Destroy

You and your party fight through Kelpwater Cove level by level, dispatching or bypassing the Drowned Sailors in Tide Cut, facing down the singing Initiates in the Choir Gallery, and reaching the Vault to confront the two Drowned Choir Initiates pressing the Seraphine ledger into the black water.

**Trigger:** Hostility with the Drowned Choir, or choosing to make your way toward the Vault with the intent to fight.

**What It Requires:** Defeating or disabling the two Drowned Choir Initiates in the Vault before round 10. These foes are formidable—AC 12, 22 HP each, with tridents (+4 to hit, 1d6+2 piercing) and resistance to nonmagical damage while near the ledger. The water around you is 20 feet deep and cold. The bridge is narrow and slick. If you kill them, they do not rise again.

**Outcome if Successful:** The ledger remains in your hands. Coria Sleet, seeing her work undone, does not flee—she stands in the Vault doorway, grief-stricken and furious, and fights to the last. Once she falls, the curse binding the Drowned Choir to their purpose breaks. The remaining drowned—those still guarding the levels above—collapse into stillness. Over the next few days, their bodies wash ashore on Saltrest's beaches, and the village performs a proper funeral rite. Mother Vell, watching the dead finally at rest, regains her faith and blesses the fishing boats once more. The nets, within a week, begin to fill with fish again.

**Reward:** Coria's spellbook (a waterlogged tome containing *raise dead*, *speak with dead*, and *dispel magic*, among other necromancy spells) and a signet ring she wore—a relic of her wife, the Seraphine's lost captain. Mayor Halrik, freed from the binding curse but still drowned and hollow-eyed, is brought back to his widow. He lives, but he is not the man he was. He remembers the Vault. He remembers the ledger sinking. This memory will haunt him for the rest of his life, and Saltrest will have to choose whether to accept him as their leader or turn him away. The village's fishing industry recovers fully within a season.

**Consequence:** The mercy path closes forever. The 143 names on the Seraphine ledger will never be read aloud. The dead do rest—but they rest forgotten, as they have for seventeen years. The ledger itself, once recovered, begins to decay rapidly in sunlight and air. Within a month, it is illegible. Within a year, it is dust. This is a victory, but it is not without its cost—the dead are denied the final witness Coria believed they deserved.

### Path 2 — Town Defense / Hold the Breakwater

You do not pursue the Drowned Choir into Kelpwater Cove. Instead, you learn from Mother Vell or your own investigation that the Choir is strong but not infinite—that Coria's primary goal is the ledger, not conquest of Saltrest. You choose to defend the village and wait for the drowned to come ashore at the breakwater.

**Trigger:** Choosing to fortify Saltrest, organize the fishing families and guards, and prepare a defensive line at the breakwater where the drowned are most likely to emerge.

**What It Requires:** A successful DC 13 Charisma (Persuasion) check to convince Mayor Halrik's widow and the village militia captain to trust your plan. You must also decide how to handle Mother Vell—if you bring her into the defense, even in a non-combat role (blessing the breakwater, praying for the village's safety), the morale of Saltrest's people increases, and you gain advantage on rolls to steady the line when the drowned arrive. The confrontation will happen at the breakwater on the fifth night of your wait.

**Outcome if Successful:** On that fifth night, seventy-three of the Drowned Choir emerge from the cove and begin walking toward Saltrest proper. They are not fast—they move with the jerking, deliberate gait of things pulled from deep water. They do not run. They do not scream. They sing. Your defensive line holds. The party, positioned on the breakwater's high stones, can rain ranged attacks from above. The drowned are slow to climb. Some of your defenders fall—a fisherman, one of the militia—but the Choir does not reach the village proper. After an hour of combat, the Drowned Choir's singers falter. The spell holding them to purpose begins to unravel. They stop advancing. They simply stand in the shallow water, singing, as if waiting for something. If the party investigates, they realize: the ledger in the Vault is sinking. The Choir senses its dissolution. Their binding frays. By morning, the few remaining Drowned who came ashore have walked back into the sea and do not return. Coria, sensing the end, does not emerge from Kelpwater Cove.

**Reward:** The village hails your party as saviors. The immediate threat passes. Saltrest is safe, and its fishing industry, though wounded, begins a slow recovery. Each party member is offered a permanent room at the Salted Anchor, a modest annual stipend from the village coffers, and the rank of "Defender of Saltrest" (which grants advantage on Charisma checks when introducing yourselves in coastal communities). Mayor Halrik's widow offers the signet ring Halrik wore—a family heirloom of Saltrest's founding—as a token of gratitude. Mother Vell, her faith restored by the village's survival, offers blessings and a small crystal shaped like a tear-drop, which grants advantage on one saving throw related to fear or despair (usable once, then loses its magic).

**Consequence:** The Seraphine ledger dissolves in the Vault in the silence and dark. Coria, alone with her failure, eventually vanishes into the deep caves. She is not seen again. The 143 drowned names are never spoken aloud. They are denied the final mercy Coria believed she was offering. The remaining Drowned Choir do not rise again, but they do not rest in peace—they are merely gone, their purpose unfinished. Saltrest survives, but so does the haunting: on quiet nights, some fishers swear they can still hear the Choir's singing, faint and fading, from the direction of Kelpwater Cove. The song will grow quieter each year, but it will not stop for a generation.

### Path 3 — Mercy / Read the Ledger Aloud

You race into Kelpwater Cove not to destroy the Choir, but to recover the Seraphine ledger before it dissolves and read the 143 names aloud. This is the most desperate path—it requires understanding Coria's true purpose, believing (at least partly) that her mercy is genuine, and beating the clock before the ledger is lost forever.

**Trigger:** Learning that the ledger can grant the dead peace, that Coria's motive is grief and not malice, and choosing to retrieve and read it before round 10 of the sinking curse.

**What It Requires:** First, you must reach the Vault. This means navigating or defeating the Drowned Sailors in Tide Cut (AC 11, 11 HP each; they do not pursue beyond their chamber and do not pursue relentlessly if you retreat) and moving past or negotiating with the Drowned Initiates in the Choir Gallery (AC 12, 8 HP each; they will stand aside if you approach them with respect and show no hostility—a successful DC 14 Charisma (Persuasion or Insight) check suffices). Once in the Vault, you must cross the bridge and reach the platform without falling into the 20-foot-deep black water. The bridge is slick (DC 12 Dexterity (Acrobatics) to cross at more than half speed without falling prone). Time is critical: the ledger is visible and recoverable for rounds 1–4 of the sinking curse. At round 5, the ledger is half-submerged, and recovery requires a **DC 15 Strength (Athletics)** check. At rounds 6–9, it sinks deeper, the check increases to **DC 17**, and many pages are already lost. At round 10, the ledger touches the bottom, the pages dissolve, and this path closes.

The two Drowned Choir Initiates in the Vault (AC 12, 22 HP each) will not stop you if you convince them you intend to read the names—another DC 14 Charisma (Persuasion or Insight) check. If they believe you, they will pause their sinking curse for 1 round, giving you precious time to recover the ledger. If you fail to convince them or fight them, they will resume sinking the ledger faster, and the clock accelerates (the sinking advances by 2 rounds if both initiates are actively hostile). Mayor Halrik, one of the binding cursed, will recognize you if you have spoken to his widow. A successful DC 13 Wisdom (Insight) check allows you to understand that some part of him is trapped in the curse and does not wish to be there. He will not help—he cannot—but he will not actively hinder you.

**Outcome if Successful:** You retrieve the Seraphine ledger before round 10. You escape Kelpwater Cove (this may require further negotiation with the Choir or a final evasion). You return to Saltrest with the manifest. Coria does not follow—she remains in the Vault, broken and grieving. That evening, as the sun sets, you gather the village—Mother Vell, the fishing families, the militia, anyone who will listen. You stand on the breakwater and read the 143 names aloud, slowly and carefully, from the waterlogged pages of the Seraphine ledger. With each name, something settles in the water. The air warms slightly. The singing that has haunted Saltrest's nights grows quieter, then ceases. By the time you reach the last name, the sea is utterly still. The Drowned Choir does not rise that night, nor any night after. They have been given their final witness. They are at rest.

**Reward:** The Seraphine ledger itself, now a historical relic of Saltrest, grants you **three blessings of the Drowned**—each usable once, each granting advantage on one Constitution (Endurance) check related to water, drowning, or cold. The village offers a statue carved in your honor, erected on the breakwater. Mother Vell, her faith strengthened and her god affirmed in her belief, offers a personal blessing and grants you a **Water-Saint's Amulet** (a minor magical item that grants advantage on saving throws against being frightened or charmed while within sight of the sea or a large body of water). The fishing families, grateful and awestruck, give you the best catch of each season's first haul for a year (a modest but genuine gesture of gratitude). Mayor Halrik, freed from his binding curse the moment the last name is read, collapses on the beach. He is alive but traumatized. He will carry the memory of the Vault and the drowned for the rest of his life, but he is himself again—and he remembers that you chose mercy.

**Consequence:** Coria Sleet, her purpose fulfilled but her grief unresolved, walks deeper into Kelpwater Cove and does not emerge. The Drowned Sailors and Initiates return to the waters and to stillness. Kelpwater Cove becomes, once more, simply a place—haunted by memory, but not by active malice. The sea begins to be kind to Saltrest's fishers again. The nets begin to fill. But the ledger, having absorbed the seawater for seventeen years, continues to decay. The names, once read aloud, are lost to sight forever. Future generations of Saltrest will know that 143 souls were carried home, but they will not know their names. This is the cost of mercy—the dead are at peace, but the living must carry the burden of memory for them. This path offers the most moving resolution, but it is also the most ephemeral. The village will build songs and stories around what the party did, but the proof—the ledger itself—will eventually turn to dust. The dead are saved, but they are saved in silence.
